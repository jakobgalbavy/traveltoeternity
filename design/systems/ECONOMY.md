# Economy & Progression

## Mission Economy

### Payout Formula (draft)
```
payout = base_rate × distance_multiplier × crew_survival_rate × cargo_integrity × reputation_bonus
```

- **base_rate:** Fixed per destination (set by the colonization market)
- **distance_multiplier:** Linear or exponential scaling with light-years; long-haul missions multiply base 5–20×
- **crew_survival_rate:** (crew alive at landing / crew at departure) — incentivizes keeping people alive
- **cargo_integrity:** Settlers delivered + cargo condition
- **reputation_bonus:** Increases with successful missions; unlocks premium contracts

### Costs
- Ship maintenance & fuel (pre-launch, scales with distance)
- Crew salaries
- Supplies (food, medicine, spare parts, seeds)
- Insurance (optional; mitigates loss but cuts payout)
- Emergency resupply (mid-voyage drone drop — expensive, limited range)

## Progression

### Unlocks via Reputation
| Reputation tier | Unlocks |
|----------------|---------|
| Novice | Short-range destinations only |
| Proven | Mid-range; better crew types available |
| Veteran | Long-range; ship upgrades; colonist specialists |
| Elite | Extreme distance; legendary destinations; secret routes |

### Ship Upgrades
Persistent upgrades that carry between missions:

- Hull reinforcement (less damage from impacts)
- Expanded grow bays (more food capacity)
- Medical bay upgrade (better illness outcomes)
- Redundant life support (slower system degradation)
- Crew quarters upgrade (morale bonus)
- Better sensors (earlier event warnings)

### Crew Development
Crew who survive missions gain experience → skill increases → better event outcomes. Attachment forms — losing a veteran crew member hurts more than losing a recruit.

---

## Risk Levers (player control)
- Take insurance or not
- Overprovision supplies (costly, safer) vs. lean (risky, more profit)
- Rush the voyage (higher engine stress, faster payout) vs. cruise (slower, safer)
- Accept distress calls or not (cost vs. reputation vs. extra people)
